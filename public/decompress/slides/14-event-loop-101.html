Event Loop 101 — two queues

Microtask queue
  • What: .then() callbacks, await resumptions, queueMicrotask, process.nextTick
  • Cost: ~free — pure JS, no syscall
  • Drained: fully, between every macrotask
Macrotask queue (libuv phases)
  • What: I/O completion callbacks, timers, setImmediate, close events
  • Cost: at least one syscall + thread-pool round trip
  • Drained: one task per phase per loop turn

Golden rule Between two macrotasks the microtask queue is drained to empty. So when an I/O completes, its .then() chain runs synchronously until it hits the next async I/O.

Mental model An I/O syscall is a checkpoint; everything between two checkpoints is one atomic burst of microtasks.
