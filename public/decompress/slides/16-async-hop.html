The unit of speed: the async hop

An async hop = one round-trip through libuv. Conceptually:

submit → thread runs syscall → completion signal → callback fires → microtasks drain

1 async hop ~tens of µs on warm cache — dominated by thread sync, not the syscall itself
N .then() in a row
(no I/O) ~free — runs in the same microtask drain, ~hundreds of ns total
Inter-chain races decided not by syscall durations but by how many hops you sit through before your write

If chain A has one fewer I/O than chain B before reaching its critical syscall, A wins by ~one full hop — orders of magnitude more than scheduler jitter.
