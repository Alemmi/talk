The asymmetry — what the disk sees

t = 0 decompress() called
dist/   (empty) /tmp/pwned   (does not exist)
t ≈ 4 · Tio S6: symlink(2) completes
dist/   aaaaa → /tmp/pwned /tmp/pwned   (still does not exist)
t ≈ 5 · Tio F6: open(2) follows the symlink
dist/   aaaaa → /tmp/pwned /tmp/pwned   "ARBITRARY WRITE"

The kill Between t ≈ 4·Tio and t ≈ 5·Tio, the filesystem holds a symlink at the exact path the file chain is about to write. The check on F4 ran a hop earlier, when there was nothing there.

Reliability The exploit is not winning a race — it is exploiting a structural inequality of async hops. Every run, every host, every filesystem.
