Why this is deterministic

Let T_io be the wall-clock cost of a single async hop — submit, syscall, completion signal, callback fire, microtask drain. Empirically tens of microseconds for a cached readlink/realpath.

F reaches F6 5 · T_io after start
(F1, F2, F3, F4, F5)
S reaches S6 4 · T_io after start
(S1, S2, S3, S5)
Gap ~1 · T_io
orders of magnitude bigger than scheduler jitter

For F to win the race, F's open(2) would have to complete before S's symlink(2). But S's symlink(2) is submitted before F's open(2) is even scheduled. The submission gap alone covers the entire syscall.

Conclusion This isn't a probabilistic race — it's a structural inequality of async hops. The exploit fires every single time on every Linux/macOS host with a hot or cold filesystem.
