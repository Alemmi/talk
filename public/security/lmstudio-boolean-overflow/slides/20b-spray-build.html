Building the 4 GB — the spray is KV metadata

what we append to the GGUF KV section
s.000000 : UINT8[ 65504 ]  ┐
s.000001 : UINT8[ 65504 ]  │  each value = our chosen
s.000002 : UINT8[ 65504 ]  │  bytes, repeated to
   ...                     │  fill the whole chunk
s.063999 : UINT8[ 65504 ]  ┘
──────────────────────────────────────────
 ~64,000  ×  65,504 B  ≈  4.19 GB sprayed

No special channel — the spray is just tens of thousands of ordinary KV entries with unique keys (s.000000…), in the same metadata block as block_count.

Why it must be ~4 GB The heap starts low (non-PIE) and 0x100000001 sits at the ~4 GiB mark. Only by allocating that much do the chunks grow contiguously up to the target — spray less and we never reach it.

Why uniform ~64 KiB chunks One multi-GB value would land in an unpredictable mmap region. Many equal allocations of 65,504 B (value + allocator overhead = a clean ~64 KiB chunk) instead pack back-to-back — gap-free, predictable coverage.

Why UINT8 arrays The bytes we plant are full of NULs; a string KV would truncate at the first one. A UINT8 array stores every byte verbatim.
